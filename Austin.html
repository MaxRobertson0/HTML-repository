The high obesity rates particularly in Texas have major social and urban causes

(not as unhealthy as obesity) This is an extremely high proportion of people as, for comparison, the obesity
rate in france is 17%. This high rate is caused by many factors which we will see throughout this web page. many of the factors are shared between all of the cities
on the map as they share many flaws in their construction, social circumstances,
and general mindset of the population towards how they live

As we can see, Austin is a city with a lot of crime, although it is not as bad as the other cities featured on the web site like Dallas, Houston etc and is even considered by some to be safe by the Austin Chronicle despite being in the top 30 cities with the most crime. What is the realtion between crime and obesity?At first I didnt know either but when you think about it it starts to make sense. What are the main waysto become healthier? Exercise like going for a run or simply even walking to the store instead ofdriving there. However with such a high crime rate, would you feel safe walking everywhere, would you feel safe going for a run in the risky parts of town? I certainly wouldn't and a lot of people have the same mindset limiting their ability to get daily exercise worsening the obesity problem.




As seen in the images at the top of the page, Austin is a city with major highways entering the centre
Without any problems. This is a vast contrast to european cities where highways are kept out of the city center
which is done to avoid both air and sound pollution from these cars. However, Autin is nowhere near
as car centric as Houston and they have less enormous highways despite being the "capital" of Texas.
Highways don't seem to have a lot in common with obesity however the presence of these large roads
Encourages people to drive everywhere as it is seen as the main mode of transportation leading to
less people walking to walkable destinations. Aditionally, many routes in a walkable distance are not
safely walkable beacuse streets are often prioritised for cars not people.




Austin is a city with major economic diversity and is a prime example of the urban sprawl that leads to the rich moving to the suburbs leaving the poor in the inner city. Poverty is arguably one of the biggest factors leading towards obesity which is a bit counter intuitive as you would think that people who have more money to buy food would be more likely to become obese through over consumption. However, according to my research the rich are often less obese than the poor. This is due to the fact that poor people have less access to better quality foods like fresh vegetables and instead turn towards cheaper alternatives like fast food or processed foods which are extremely unhealthy when consumed on a regular basis. We can therefore see that obesity disproportianally affects the most vulnerable in US society which is why it needs to be fixed.




Potential solutions

-Making cities safer to encourage more exercise within the population
-Either (somehow) decreasing inequality between economic classes in the US so that veryone can afford better quality goods or
Making the better quality foods more affordable so that even the lower classes can access them instead of resorting to fast foods
-Decreasing car dependancy within the city and its suburbs through walkable cities and public transit for longer journeys like
frequent buses/trams that are reliable I.E. reducing car dependancy
